The architectural style of the Taj Mahal is a magnificent blend of Persian, Islamic, and Indian influences, showcasing the height of Mughal architectural innovation. The central structure is crowned by a large onion-shaped dome, nearly 35 meters high, flanked by four smaller domes. Surrounding the main tomb are four slender minarets, each standing about 40 meters tall, which are slightly tilted outward to protect the tomb in case of a collapse. The entire complex is set within expansive formal gardens, laid out in the traditional Persian Charbagh style, symbolizing paradise on earth. These gardens are divided by walkways and water channels that reflect the monument, enhancing its symmetrical beauty.
